Split Fiction

Description

As with Hazelight's previous game, It Takes Two, Split Fiction was specifically designed for split screen cooperative multiplayer, which means that it must be played with another player through either local or online play. In the game, players assume control of either Zoe or Mio, two authors who both become trapped inside their own stories after being linked to a machine. The two strangers must work together to escape and overcome numerous challenges along the way, as Mio's science fiction story interweaves with Zoe's fantasy story, putting both characters in grave danger

Reviews of Game(Shorten)

IGN: Split Fiction is a fast, constantly shifting co‑op adventure where two clashing writers, Zoe and Mio, get trapped inside a tech company’s system that’s been exploiting their work. The game fires off genre after genre—platforming, shooters, sports, puzzles—without ever slowing down, and most of those ideas land with style. Its bosses are imaginative, its worlds swing from neon sci‑fi to dark fantasy, and the finale stands out as one of the reviewer’s most memorable. And it an expertly crafted, endlessly inventive co‑op experience that never stops surprising

Metacritic: Split Fiction is a heartfelt cooperative adventure that celebrates friendship and teamwork with consistently engaging mechanics that never overstay their welcome. Despite minor flaws, its impressive level design and spectacular final sequence make it a standout experience best played with a friend. Hazelight Studios has once again pushed the boundaries of the co-op genre—leaving us eager to see what they’ll create next.

PC Gamer: Split Fiction is described as a wild, rapid‑fire co‑op adventure that never stops changing what it is. The game constantly hurls you into new genres—fantasy quests, sci‑fi shooters, physics toys, sports challenges, puzzle rooms—all delivered with the speed and chaos of a theme‑park ride. The characters themselves are flat and predictable, but the nonstop creativity, clever teamwork moments, and high‑stakes set pieces make the experience shine. And the finale one of the coolest co‑op endings they’ve ever played, sealing it as a relentlessly inventive two‑player journey.

Age Rating

PEGI 16 (Europe): The game is not suitable for players under 16 years old. This rating is due to graphic violence against fantasy characters, such as trolls and dragons, and realistic-looking violence toward human characters, including impalement and blood effects. The game also contains moderate horror sequences, disturbing imagery, and menacing voices that may be frightening to younger players.

ESRB Teen (T, North America): Rated for ages 13 and older, with content descriptors including Blood and Gore, Crude Humor, Language, and Violence. Combat features blood-splatter effects, cries of pain, and boss battles depicting decapitation or impalement. The game also includes mild to moderate profanity and crude humor, such as pigs using flatulence and monkeys throwing feces.

IMDA Advisory 16 (Singapore): Suitable for ages 16 and above, highlighting moderate violence, bloodshed, and occasional strong language. Combat sequences involve weapons like swords, whips, and spells, with story cutscenes showing characters being impaled, severed body parts, and blood splatter.
