Description

A Way Out is designed exclusively for split-screen cooperative play, either locally or online, and cannot be played solo. Players control two protagonists, Leo Caruso and Vincent Moretti, who must escape from prison and evade authorities while pursuing personal goals. The game is played from a third-person perspective, and the story unfolds simultaneously for both characters, meaning one player may be actively controlling their character while the other watches a cutscene. Players must cooperate to solve puzzles, complete objectives, and progress through the story, with roles that can vary in different playthroughs.

Reviews of Game(Shorten)

IGN: A Way Out is a six‑hour, story‑driven co‑op adventure that blends drama, action, and humor through the intertwined journeys of Vincent and Leo. And the how the game constantly shifts its gameplay stealth, brawling, driving, minigames creating variety even if none of the mechanics feel as refined as dedicated titles. The split‑screen presentation lets both players explore freely, and the contrasting personalities of the two leads shape dialogue, choices, and how scenes unfold. That the game’s cinematic ambition shines in standout sequences like the one shot hospital escape and the cooperative back to back climb. Some moments feel implausible or clunky, especially the gunplay, but the emotional payoff of the finale makes the entire experience worth completing. The mandatory co‑op isn’t a gimmick; it’s the core of what makes the story and gameplay work.

Metacritic: A Way Out is a true achievement in game design. Not only does it manage to take players on a true journey of emotion, it does so in a way that is only possible through the medium of video games. It manages to do something truly special by making two players to cooperate for an extended amount of time, and the discussions that happen on the couch or over voice chat can be just as revealing as the game itself. Josef Fares and Hazelight have undoubtedly raised the bar for co-op gaming.

GameRader+: A Way Out is praised as a rare game that fully commits to co‑op, requiring two players and building every mechanic, scene, and story beat around teamwork. GamesRadar+ highlights how the game creates a true shared narrative, where progress depends on constant communication and coordinated actions. Even small interactions like passing tools, distracting guards, or playing minigames—feel meaningful because they reinforce the bond between Leo and Vincent.The game’s innovative split‑screen cinematics, which shift dynamically to keep both players engaged and make the experience feel cinematic. Across its roughly ten‑hour story, the pacing stays tight, blending narrative moments, puzzles, stealth, and action sequences reminiscent of Uncharted. The only drawbacks noted are the similar character designs and a QTE‑heavy opening, but these don’t overshadow the overall experience.

Age Rating

PEGI Rating (Europe): Rates the game 18+, restricting it to adults. This rating is legally enforced in Europe and reflects the game's graphic violence, sexual content, nudity, and pervasive profanity. Selling the game to anyone under 18 is prohibited.

ESRB Rating (North America): Gave A Way Out an M for Mature, indicating it is suitable for players 17 years and older. This rating is due to blood, intense violence, sexual content, nudity, strong language, and drug use throughout the game. The game includes scenes of physical fights, emotional and intense moments, and some graphic violence, such as torture sequences. Strong language like "fuck," "shit," and "motherfucker" is frequently used.
