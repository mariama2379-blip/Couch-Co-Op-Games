Description

Reanimal is a survival horror game, stealth game, and a cinematic platformer. The story follows a brother and sister as they try to escape a hellish version of their home while attempting to save their three friends. The two must work together, explore the islands, escape from monsters, and solve various environmental puzzles in order to progress. Players will eventually unlock a boat which enables them to explore locations off the critical path in a nonlinear manner. The game supports both single-player and two-player online or local cooperative multiplayer. In single-player, the second companion is controlled by artificial intelligence.

Reviews of Game(Shorten)

IGN: Reanimal tells a quiet, eerie story about two children crossing a foggy sea and searching for lost friends. Its strength is the atmosphere—fixed camera shots, no HUD, and striking visuals that make every scene feel intentional. The puzzles are simple but satisfying, and the emotional impact comes from playing with another person, not an AI partner. As the world grows stranger and more disturbing, the game leans into horror without explaining too much, letting the imagery speak for itself. A few chase sequences and bugs can frustrate, but the six‑hour journey stays compelling because of its mystery, mood, and the bond between the two characters.

Metacritic: Thanks to a wonderful concept, exceptional level design, well-thought-out mechanics that are executed even better, and a spectacular audiovisual presentation that creates a deep atmosphere you don’t want to leave, Reanimal stands as one of the most memorable experiences of 2026 so far—one that we believe already deserves a secured place on the lists of the year’s very best. Tarsier Studios proves that they truly understand their craft better than anyone, and that the wisest course of action is simply to let them work, so all that immense creativity can flourish and be brought to life for all of us to enjoy.

Game Spot: Reanimal follows two orphaned siblings as they search hostile islands for their missing friends, creating a tense journey defined by grotesque creatures, muted gray environments, and sharp flashes of red that signal danger. The atmosphere is its strongest element—constant unease, eerie sound cues, and the sense that something is always watching.The story uses symbolism and fragmented lore, pushing players to interpret its trauma‑centered themes themselves. Co‑op is essential, making coordination and staying close feel meaningful. Gameplay mirrors Little Nightmares with stealth, simple puzzles, and occasional weapon use, though the puzzles rarely challenge and weaken pacing. Despite this, the narrative twists and visual set pieces make it easy to finish in one sitting.
