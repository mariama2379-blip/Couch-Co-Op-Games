Description

It Takes Two follows Cody and May, a couple on the brink of divorce who are magically transformed into dolls by their daughter’s wish for them to reconcile. Trapped in a fantastical world shaped by their strained relationship, they must work together to return to their human bodies. Each level introduces new, story driven abilities—such as rewinding time or creating duplicates—that require tight cooperation to overcome challenges. Blending action adventure and platforming with constantly shifting mechanics, the game emphasizes communication, teamwork, and emotional growth as the pair confront their shared history and rediscover what once connected them. Along the way, playful minigames and inventive scenarios reinforce the theme that repairing a relationship takes effort from both partners.

Reviews of Game(Shorten)

IGN: It Takes Two is described as an “utterly superb co‑op platformer” packed with “unique and exhilarating gameplay ideas” that never repeat. IGN praises how the game constantly shifts styles—from dungeon crawls to flying planes to kaleidoscope platforming—calling it a “10‑hour-long blast of co‑op platforming bliss.” The controls are “supremely responsive,” and each chapter gives Cody and May new, complementary abilities that turn simple tasks into coordinated teamwork moments. The world is full of playful, interactive details added simply “because it’s fun,” and generous checkpoints encourage experimentation. The main complaint is Dr. Hakim, the “insufferably cringy” Book of Love, though his appearances usually mean new abilities. The story of Cody and May reconciling is seen as charming, even if the ending feels “slightly saccharine.”And that It Takes Two is a “beautiful, breakneck‑paced, co‑op adventure bubbling over with creativity,” and strongly recommends it for anyone with a co‑op partner.

Metacritic: It Takes Two is a fun cooperative game on the PlayStation that is filled with consistent surprises and delights, offering a unique and unforgettable experience. Whether you’re playing with a significant other, a friend or a family member, It Takes Two is an essential title for any PS5 owner who is looking for a cooperative challenge and from an incredible moving perspective from a story point of view that can easily be knocked over in a few binge gaming sessions.

Polygon: It Takes Two “stranger and more unsettling than its elevator pitch,” mixing “gun‑wielding squirrels,” “stuffed animal torture,” and a “paper couples counselor” into a story that feels like a “TGIF sitcom” with “childish and simplistic” ideas about marriage. The game swings between kid‑friendly humor and moments “so adult and dark” that it’s unclear who it’s for, with the stuffed‑elephant scene highlighted as especially disturbing. The co‑op gameplay is stronger, with constant shifts in mechanics and paired abilities that work best when the game “moves quickly from one style of play to the next.” But the second half drags with “redundant tasks,” and the talking book becomes a “symbol of dread.” And that the game is full of “inexplicable creative decisions,” mixing absurd brilliance with a weak, misguided narrative that never earns its emotional payoff.

Age Rating

PEGI 12 — Moderate fantasy violence and occasional mild language. The violence is cartoon‑like, with toy characters breaking apart instead of showing injuries. Some scenes show characters being pinned or harmed, but everything is presented in a whimsical, non‑graphic way.

ESRB Teen (13+) — Includes animated blood, comic mischief, fantasy violence, and mild language. Players see cartoon combat, explosions, purple blood effects, and occasional words like “sh*t,” along with silly humor such as flatulence jokes.
